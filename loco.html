LOCO

Navigate Public Transportation Like a Local

powered by

Background

It always an adventure when exploring a new city. Taking public transportation is the mode of travel that many travelers choose, but each city has a different system. Travelers cannot rely entirely on their original experience. It is even more challenging if they travel to another country that uses a different language, they might get overwhelmed by the subway map.

In this project, the target is independent travelers, backpackers. They use public transportation and plan their trip by themselves.

Discovery

Wayfinding experience

The traveler is at A station and want to go to B station, he has to search stations on the metro network and decide which line to take or use navigation app. Next, when transfer, he has to search where another line is. Last, when leave the station, he has to search the exit number on the list and follow it. The whole journey is to search again and again.

Check the metro network and decide the line

Search another line when transfer

Search the exit number on the list

Follow the exit signs to reach the destination

Problems

I observed the behavior of target travelers and found some problems that they face during their journey.

Travelers hesitate when get off the train or in front of the gate because they want to reach their destination but they don’t want to make mistakes.

Even though there are many signs in the station, travelers still double check with the staff. They doubt the signs because they want to make sure that they are going in the correct direction.

And many travelers don’t understand Japanese and English. As a result, language barrier might stop them from getting to their destination.

Results

Strongest Need:

Travelers want to reach their destination.

Insight:

If travelers can become locals, then they can reach their destination without any problem.

Concept:

A product that can make traveler get around like a local in public transit.

The traveler simply connects LOCO to their smartphone, set a destination and let it show the way.

Someone might say that this problem can easily solve by a the smartphone. Maybe they are right, but it is very dangerous. Research by NTT Docomo estimates a pedestrian's average field of vision while staring down at a smartphone is just 5% of what our eyes take in normally. Therefore, we can see a lot of warning posters that tell us don't use smartphone while walking.

But with loco, we don’t have to focus on a smartphone because the vertical range of the visual field is around 120 degrees and 80 degrees below the horizontal meridian. We can still see our shoes out of the corner of our eye when we look straight ahead.
